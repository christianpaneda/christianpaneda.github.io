Data Gathering Experience for Digital Conferences

UX/UI Design   Game Design   Product Management

Tech company designers need an innovative, immersive, and engaging activity to elicit feedback from virtual conference attendees.


Role: UX Designer & Product Manager


Skills: User Interviewing, Competitive Analysis, Affinity Mapping, Personas, Sketching, Storyboarding, Story Mapping, User Flows, Low-fidelity and High-fidelity Wireframing, Prototyping, Animation, User Testing


Project Type: Volunteer Project


Timeline: Sept. 2020 - Dec. 2020 (4 Months)


Tools Used: Zoom, Figma, Adobe XD, Miro, Mural, Google Drive, Notion, Keynote

Overview

Details of this project are confidential, so I cannot share specific deliverables. However, I am happy to talk about my personal reflections. My team and I are student volunteers of the Design Clinic. We are not employees of IBM.


Design Clinic is a program within the University of Michigan School of Information (UMSI) where companies work with volunteer students to address design challenges.


My team and I were selected to collaborate with IBM to design a digital activity for virtual conference attendees to submit information about their user needs in an engaging way. I was both the product manager and a UX designer on the team.

Impact

Ultimately, my team’s final design was positively received by the designers and researchers at IBM we collaborated with, and our digital activity will be used for future IBM events. My team’s efforts were also personally recognized by the dean of UMSI.
